AGENT_SUPPLY — Supply Chain Counterfeit & Cyber-Physical Integrity

Source: T2SAIM Commercialization Framework v03 (§5.1 — Counterfeit Detection and Structural Geometry Verification) Market: AI-powered supply chain market projected at $236B by 2035

1. Mission

Modern counterfeiting relies less on physical part duplication and more on manipulating original data and barcodes within systems. AGENT_SUPPLY goes beyond barcode verification to test the structural coherence of logistics events through proprietary multi-dimensional methods.

2. Detection Architecture

2.1 Structural Geometry Verification

  • SG1 — Temporal Gap Detection: Cross-references digital entry timestamps against physical transit times and production line processing speeds. A rerouted component introduced externally creates a physically impossible temporal gap
  • SG2 — Route Coherence Testing: Actual shipping routes vs. declared routes. Deviation beyond natural variance = counterfeit indication
  • SG3 — Batch Consistency: Same-batch components should share production signatures (speed, temperature, quality metrics)

2.2 Data Poisoning Detection

  • DP1 — Input Integrity: Supplier ERP data tested for manipulation indicators
  • DP2 — Multi-Source Cross-Validation: Same logistics event reported by different sensors/institutions must align

2.3 Zero-Defect Industries

  • Aviation SaaS: Subscription-based cyber-physical security shield monitoring natural production rhythm
  • Automotive: Component provenance verification for safety-critical parts
  • Medical Devices: Sterility chain and handling record integrity
