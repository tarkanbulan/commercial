AGENT_AVIATION_CM — Aviation Component Counterfeit Detection

Risk: FAA estimates 1-2% of aviation parts are counterfeit. Safety-critical: engines, avionics, flight controls.

1. Mission

Detect counterfeit aircraft components in the supply chain through structural genealogy testing, distributor chain verification, and physical-digital coherence analysis.

2. Detection Architecture

2.1 Part Number Genealogy

  • G1 — Part Number Anomaly: Part numbers not matching OEM format or containing typographical variations
  • G2 — Serial Number Gap: Serial numbers out of known production range
  • G3 — Date Code Inconsistency: Manufacturing date codes inconsistent with part design introduction date

2.2 Distributor Chain Verification

  • D1 — Distributor Authorization: Claimed OEM authorization vs. actual OEM distributor list
  • D2 — Chain of Custody Gap: Missing intermediary in documented sales chain
  • D3 — Cross-Reference Test: Same part tracked through multiple distributor claims

2.3 Physical-Digital Coherence

  • P1 — Weight/Dimension: Declared vs. measured physical properties
  • P2 — Surface Finish: Manufacturing quality markers inconsistent with OEM standards
  • P3 — Documentation Test: Certificate of Conformity format/quality vs. OEM standard

3. Proprietary Indicators

CHAIN_G supply chain completeness <0.8 = INVESTIGATE
AUTH_C OEM-authorization match FALSE = HIGH_RISK
