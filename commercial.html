I. Commercial Domain

ESG Compliance, Supply Chain Integrity & Knowledge Process Outsourcing

Post-Brexit UK is developing its own ESG regulatory framework, diverging from EU directives. The UK's Green Taxonomy, carbon border adjustment mechanism (CBAM-UK), and expanded Modern Slavery Act reporting requirements create a compliance ecosystem valued at £2.1B by 2027.

Three distinct service lines operate within this domain:

1. ESG Compliance (DPP)

The UK's departure from EU ESG frameworks necessitates a parallel domestic compliance infrastructure. Key drivers include:

  • UK Green Taxonomy: A classification system for sustainable economic activities, diverging from the EU Taxonomy. Companies must report alignment from 2026.
  • CBAM-UK: Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism targeting imports from jurisdictions with weaker climate policies. Forensic verification of embedded carbon emissions required.
  • Modern Slavery Act 2015: Expanded reporting requirements from 2025, covering supply chain transparency statements for companies with £36M+ turnover.
  • Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures (TCFD): Mandatory from April 2025 for UK companies.

Service lines include: supply chain due diligence, carbon footprint verification, human rights auditing, and regulatory compliance reporting.

2. Knowledge Process Outsourcing (KnowPro)

UK legal and financial services firms are increasingly outsourcing complex analytical work. The market for outsourced KYC/AML due diligence, litigation support research, and regulatory reporting analysis is growing at 18% CAGR. Key clients include:

  • London law firms (Magic Circle and mid-tier) requiring forensic research for cross-border litigation
  • Financial institutions needing outsourced AML/KYC verification
  • Regulatory compliance teams requiring specialist analytical capacity

3. Supply Chain Integrity

UK supply chains face three simultaneous disruptions: post-Brexit customs friction, net-zero transition costs, and geopolitical rerouting. Integrity verification — from raw material provenance to final mile delivery — represents a £1.1B opportunity.

  • Border Target Operating Model (BTOM): UK's new border controls create verification requirements for sanitary/phytosanitary goods, customs declarations, and rules of origin.
  • Net-zero supply chains: Scope 3 emissions verification throughout supply chains.
  • Geopolitical rerouting: Supply chain resilience auditing for companies affected by Ukraine conflict, Red Sea disruption, and US-China trade tensions.
