I. Commercial Domain

ESG Compliance, Supply Chain Integrity & Knowledge Process Outsourcing

Three distinct agents operate within this domain:

Agent: ESG Compliance (DPP)

Post-Brexit UK is developing its own ESG regulatory framework, diverging from EU directives. The UK's Green Taxonomy, carbon border adjustment mechanism (CBAM-UK), and expanded Modern Slavery Act reporting requirements create a compliance ecosystem valued at £2.1B by 2027. Key service lines include supply chain due diligence, carbon footprint verification, and human rights auditing.

Agent: Knowledge Process Outsourcing (KnowPro)

UK legal and financial services firms are increasingly outsourcing complex analytical work to specialised forensic research providers. The market for outsourced KYC/AML due diligence, litigation support research, and regulatory reporting analysis is growing at 18% CAGR.

Agent: Supply Chain Integrity

UK supply chains face three simultaneous disruptions: post-Brexit customs friction, net-zero transition costs, and geopolitical rerouting. Integrity verification — from raw material provenance to final mile delivery — represents a £1.1B opportunity.

II. Legal Forensics

Digital Forensics, ECHR Divergence & Cross-Border Litigation Support

UK's post-Brexit legal realignment — particularly the potential departure from the European Convention on Human Rights — creates structural demand for digital forensics services. The UK is also navigating the Online Safety Act, AI regulation, and cross-border data adequacy decisions.

Agent: ECHR Digital Forensics

If the UK diverges from ECHR, a parallel digital rights framework will emerge. This creates forensic opportunities in: digital evidence standardisation, cross-border data request verification, and AI-generated content authentication. The Investigatory Powers Act 2016 already generates demand for digital forensic services.

Agent: JUNET (Judicial Network)

Cross-border judicial cooperation mechanisms post-Brexit require new verification layers. Mutual legal assistance requests, evidence admissibility standards, and international disclosure obligations create a niche forensic market.

Agent: Megafile

Large-scale document review for litigation, regulatory investigations, and public inquiries. UK public inquiries (COVID, Post Office Horizon, Infected Blood) demonstrate the scale of document processing required.

III. Financial Audit & Anti-Corruption

Offshore Audit, UK Bribery Act Compliance & Forensic Accounting

UK Bribery Act enforcement reached record levels in 2025 with total fines exceeding £1.2B. The Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023, Register of Overseas Entities, and Unexplained Wealth Orders create a dense regulatory environment. This is the highest-probability sector in the matrix.

Agent: Offshore Audit

Offshore financial structures used to obscure ownership or facilitate tax evasion are a key forensic vector. UK's Register of Overseas Entities (2022) revealed approximately £90B in previously undisclosed property ownership. Forensic analysis of trust structures, shell company networks, and cross-border fund movements represents a £900M market.

Agent: Physical Asset Audit

Physical asset verification for anti-corruption purposes: verifying government procurement, infrastructure project delivery, and asset registers. UK's National Audit Office estimates £4-6B annual fraud in public procurement.

UK Bribery Act Compliance

Section 7 of the UK Bribery Act creates strict liability for commercial organisations that fail to prevent bribery. Adequate procedures defence requires demonstrable forensic controls. Cross-border due diligence, third-party risk assessment, and transaction monitoring are the primary service lines.

V. Civil Aviation

10 Sub-Domains: Production → MRO → Supply Chain → Leasing → Operations → Safety → Finance → Digital

UK civil aviation is undergoing simultaneous regulatory, technological, and environmental transitions. Post-Brexit regulatory divergence from EASA creates unique compliance and forensic opportunities. The UK CAA's expanded role in type certification, maintenance oversight, and safety investigation generates demand across all sub-domains.

Sub-Domain: Counterfeit Parts Detection (Production)

Counterfeit and unapproved aircraft parts are a growing concern. UK CAA's Airworthiness Directives increasingly require forensic verification of part provenance. Estimated market: £90M.

Sub-Domain: MRO Integrity Forensics

Maintenance, repair, and overhaul integrity verification. Post-Brexit UK MRO providers need independent forensic audit to maintain third-country approvals. Estimated market: £140M.

Sub-Domain: Engine Forensics

Engine failure analysis, life-limited part tracking, and performance degradation forensics. UK's Rolls-Royce supply chain requires specialised forensic auditing.

Sub-Domain: EU261 Compliance Automation

UK retained EU261 (now UK261) passenger rights regulation generates significant claims management and compliance automation demand. Estimated at £220M annually.

Sub-Domain: Lease Audit & Finance Forensics

Aircraft leasing is a £50B UK industry. Lease return conditions, maintenance reserves, and redelivery compliance create ongoing forensic audit demand.

Sub-Domain: FOQA Analytics

Flight Operations Quality Assurance data analytics for safety and efficiency. UK airlines are expanding FOQA programmes; independent analytics provision is an emerging niche.
