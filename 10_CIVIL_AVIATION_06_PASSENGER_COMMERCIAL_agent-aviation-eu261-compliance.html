AGENT_AVIATION_EU261 — Passenger Compensation Integrity Module

Market: EU261 claims market estimated at €1-2B/yr. UK261 (retained) same framework. Regulatory: EC 261/2004, UK retained version (CAP 1396), CAA enforcement

1. Mission

Verify passenger compensation claim integrity. Distinguish genuine claims from fraudulent ones. Validate airline "extraordinary circumstances" defenses using operational data.

2. Detection Architecture

2.1 Delay/Cancellation Claim Verification

  • D1 — Operational Data Cross-Reference: Claimed delay vs. actual operational data (ACARS, OOOI)
  • D2 — Passenger Identity: Duplicate claimant across multiple flights on same date
  • D3 — Flight Leg Matching: Claimed flight actually operated on claimed date

2.2 Extraordinary Circumstances Testing

  • E1 — Weather Claim Validation: Claimed weather event vs. historical METAR for airport
  • E2 — ATC Restriction Validation: Claimed ATC restriction vs. Eurocontrol/NATS data
  • E3 — Bird Strike Claim: Bird strike claimed vs. wildlife strike database cross-reference

2.3 Compensation Fraud Detection

  • F1 — Pattern Detection: Same passenger filing multiple claims across different airlines
  • F2 — Document Integrity: Boarding pass/document forgery detection
  • F3 — Law Firm/Agent Fraud: Third-party claim management company patterns

3. Proprietary Indicators

Indicator Description Threshold
DELAY_V claimed_delay - actual_delay >15min = VERIFY
EXTRAORDINARY_R weather_validity / metar_data <0.8 = QUESTIONABLE
DUPLICATE_P passenger on N>1 flights same time >1 = FRAUD_INDICATOR
