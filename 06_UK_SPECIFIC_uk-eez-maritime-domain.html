UK EEZ Maritime Domain Awareness Module

Geographic Scope: UK Exclusive Economic Zone (~770,000 km²) + Overseas Territories (Gibraltar, Falklands, BIOT, etc.) Strategic Assets: Offshore oil & gas platforms, wind farms (world's largest offshore wind), subsea data cables (99% of UK data traffic), fishing stocks Threat Vectors: Russian naval activity, illegal fishing, sanctions evasion, hybrid warfare (cable cutting), people smuggling

1. Mission

UK maritime domain structural coherence testing. Cross-references AIS, radar, satellite imagery, port records, fishing licenses, and economic data to detect anomalies in UK waters. Supports Royal Navy, UK Maritime & Coastguard Agency, and Border Force.

2. Detection Architecture

2.1 AIS Manipulation Detection

  • A1 — AIS Gap Analysis: Deliberate transponder shutdown near sensitive areas (platforms, cables, military zones)
  • A2 — Identity Spoofing: MMSI number conflicts, repeated identity changes
  • A3 — Dark Ship Detection: Radar/satellite track without corresponding AIS = deliberate concealment

2.2 Fishing Activity Forensics

  • F1 — Licensing Compliance: Fishing vessel location vs. license zone. Unauthorized fishing = IUU (Illegal, Unreported, Unregulated)
  • F2 — Catch vs. Declaration: Landing data vs. vessel capacity. Systematic under-declaration
  • F3 — Transshipment Detection: Ship-to-ship transfer at sea (common sanctions evasion tactic)

2.3 Subsea Cable Integrity

  • C1 — Anchor Interaction Detection: Vessel track intersecting cable routes at suspiciously low speed
  • C2 — Trawling Pattern Analysis: Fishing gear vs. cable proximity — accidental vs. intentional
  • C3 — Repair & Maintenance Forensics: Cable break chronology vs. vessel tracks

2.4 Hydrocarbon Asset Protection

  • H1 — Stand-off Vessel Monitoring: Foreign vessels loitering near platforms without operational purpose
  • H2 — Supply Chain Forensics: Platform supply vessel patterns, crew rotation anomalies
  • H3 — Seismic Survey Analysis: Unauthorized survey activity in UK waters

2.5 Port State Control Forensics

  • P1 — Port Call Deseasonalization: Vessel visitation patterns vs. economic cycles. Deviations = risk
  • P2 — Crew Change Anomalies: Crew nationality changes, extended port stays without justification
  • P3 — Bunkering / Waste Transfer: Unusual fueling or waste disposal patterns

4. UK Data Sources

  • UKHO (UK Hydrographic Office): Charts, tidal data
  • Marine Traffic / S-100: AIS data (paid)
  • Satellite Imagery: ESA Sentinel, UKSA (free/paid)
  • MMO (Marine Management Organisation): Fishing licenses, landing data
  • Northwood HQ: Royal Navy operations (classified — open source proxy)
  • Kongsberg / exactEarth: Satellite AIS (paid)

5. Response Protocol

  • Green: Normal pattern — no action
  • Amber: Anomaly detected — Coastguard / NCA notification
  • Red: Active threat — Royal Navy tasking recommendation
