AGENT_DPP — EU Digital Product Passport & ESG Compliance Module

Source: T2SAIM Commercialization Framework v03 (§5.2 — EU Digital Product Passport and ESG Compliance) Regulatory Driver: EU Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR), progressively mandatory from 2026

1. Mission

T2SAIM's Digital Product Passport audit actor. Not merely a data entry system — an audit actor verifying data has not been manipulated. ESG data and "greenwashing" claims are subjected to structural analysis exposing sustainability declarations misaligned with physical reality.

2. Detection Architecture

2.1 DPP Structural Integrity Testing

  • D1 — Data Origin Verification: Tests whether DPP entries originate from authenticated production sensors vs. fabricated data injectors
  • D2 — Temporal Production Rhythm Testing: Cross-references digital entry timestamps against physical production line speeds. A component "produced" faster than physically possible = tampering
  • D3 — Multi-Node Consistency: Same product's passport entries across different nodes (factory, warehouse, retailer) must align. Deviation = manipulation

2.2 ESG / Greenwashing Detection

  • G1 — Claim-Physical Alignment: ESG declarations tested against energy consumption records, raw material inputs, and waste output data
  • G2 — Temporal Decoupling Detection: When sustainability claims improve dramatically but production processes remain unchanged = structural anomaly
  • G3 — Supply Chain Narrative Coherence: Multiple suppliers' ESG claims tested for unnatural narrative alignment (possible coordinated greenwashing)

2.3 Compliance Certification Infrastructure

  • C1 — EU Market Access Verification: Passport completeness and structural integrity before market entry
  • C2 — Cross-Border Consistency: Same product's passport across EU member states tested for national variation anomalies
  • C3 — Lifecycle Audit Trail: From raw material → production → distribution → recycling — cryptographic chain-of-custody

3. Proprietary Indicators
