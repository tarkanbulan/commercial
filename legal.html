II. Legal Forensics

Digital Forensics, ECHR Divergence & Cross-Border Litigation Support

UK's post-Brexit legal realignment — including potential departure from the European Convention on Human Rights — creates structural demand for digital forensics services. The Online Safety Act 2023, AI regulation, and cross-border data adequacy decisions generate ongoing forensic requirements.

1. Agent: ECHR Digital Forensics

If the UK diverges from ECHR, a parallel digital rights framework will emerge. Forensic opportunities include: digital evidence standardisation, cross-border data request verification, AI-generated content authentication, and Investigatory Powers Act compliance.

2. Agent: JUNET (Judicial Network)

Cross-border judicial cooperation mechanisms post-Brexit require new verification layers for mutual legal assistance requests, evidence admissibility standards, and international disclosure obligations.

3. Agent: Temporal (Chronology Verification)

Time-stamp verification and chronology mapping for legal proceedings. Event sequence reconstruction, alibi verification, and timeline consistency analysis using digital forensics.

4. Agent: Megafile (Document Review)

Large-scale document review for litigation, regulatory investigations, and public inquiries. UK public inquiries (COVID, Post Office Horizon, Infected Blood) demonstrate the scale of document processing required — often exceeding 10 million pages.
