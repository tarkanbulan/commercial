Market: Global MRO market $80-90B/yr (2025) Regulatory: EASA Part 145, FAA Part 43/145, UK CAA ANO Key Players: Lufthansa Technik, GE Services, Rolls-Royce, Air France KLM MRO, HAECO, ST Engineering

1. Mission

Structural integrity testing of aircraft maintenance operations. Detect phantom repairs, billing fraud, unnecessary part replacement, and record tampering in the MRO ecosystem.

2. Detection Architecture

2.1 Phantom Repair Detection

  • P1 — Labor Hour Anomaly: Claimed repair hours vs. industry standard (e.g., "4-engine C-check in 8 hours")
  • P2 — Part Consumption Ratio: Parts billed vs. parts physically consumed. Systematic over-billing = phantom
  • P3 — Task Duplication: Same repair task billed multiple times on different work orders

2.2 Unnecessary Part Replacement

  • U1 — Premature Replacement: LLP/LRU replaced well before manufacturer-recommended limits
  • U2 — Batch Pattern Detection: Same part replaced fleet-wide simultaneously (possible OEM-driven vs. genuine need)
  • U3 — Used Part Integrity: Parts claimed as "new" but exhibiting wear patterns inconsistent with zero hours

2.3 Maintenance Record Tampering

  • T1 — Timestamp Integrity: Work order timestamps vs. actual aircraft availability. Repair claimed while aircraft was flying
  • T2 — Signature Chain: Engineer authorization chain gaps or irregularities
  • T3 — Airworthiness Directive Compliance: AD compliance claimed but part/manual records inconsistent

2.4 MRO Pricing Manipulation

  • M1 — Rate Deviation: Shop rates vs. market benchmark. Systematic overpricing
  • M2 — Scope Creep Detection: "Found during inspection" repairs that follow a pattern
  • M3 — Flat Rate Manipulation: Flat rate vs. actual hour comparison per task type
