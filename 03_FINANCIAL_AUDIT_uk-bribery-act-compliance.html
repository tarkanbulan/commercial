UK Bribery Act 2010 Compliance Scanner

Regulatory Driver: UK Bribery Act 2010 — world's strictest anti-bribery legislation §7 Strict Liability: Commercial organisations liable if an "associated person" bribes on their behalf — UNLESS they have "adequate procedures" in place Enforcement: UK Serious Fraud Office (SFO), National Crime Agency (NCA) Market: All FTSE 350 + any company "carrying on business" in the UK (extraterritorial)

1. Mission

Structural audit of UK Bribery Act §7 compliance. Not document review — behavioral signature analysis of procurement networks, payment flows, and supplier relationships. Detects bribery risk without whistleblower testimony or email evidence.

2. Detection Architecture

2.1 Ghost Supplier Detection

  • G1 — Cash Flow Purpose Ratio: Internal purchasing/payment traffic — cash outflows with no rational economic purpose
  • G2 — Supplier Longevity Anomaly: Companies created days before first invoice, dissolved after payment
  • G3 — Address Clustering: Multiple "suppliers" at same registered address (often a shell farm)

2.2 Reciprocity / Quid Pro Quo Testing

  • R1 — Procurement-Decision Benefit Lag: Time between a procurement decision and a benefit to the decision-maker
  • R2 — Third-Party Intermediary Detection: Benefits routed through consultants, agents, or family members
  • R3 — Hospitality Pattern Analysis: Gifts, travel, entertainment frequency vs. contract value correlation

2.3 Section 7 Adequate Procedures Assessment

  • P1 — Procedure Existence vs. Operation: Written policy exists but behavioral indicators show non-compliance
  • P2 — Due Diligence Gap: Supplier vetting process vs. actual supplier risk profile
  • P3 — Tone-from-Top Testing: Board-level risk awareness vs. operational reality gap
  • P4 — Whistleblower Channel Effectiveness: Reported concerns vs. actual investigation rate

2.4 UK-Specific Risk Vectors

  • Foreign Public Official Interactions: Cross-border agent payments
  • Facilitation Payment Patterns: Despite UK Bribery Act zero-tolerance, structural indicators
  • Political Donation Forensics: Donations → contract award correlation at local authority level

3. Proprietary Indicators

Indicator Formula (Conceptual) Threshold
GHOST_P purpose_ratio of unexplained outflows >0.25 = HIGH_RISK
RECIP_D decision-to-benefit time lag distribution <7d OR >90d = PATTERN
ADEQUACY_G written_policy - operational_reality >0.4 = SECTION_7_RISK
SUPPLIER_L supplier_age_at_first_invoice <30d = SHELL_INDICATOR

4. UK Data Sources

  • Companies House API: Company registry, PSC register, filing history
  • UK Contracts Finder: Public procurement awards
  • SFO Prosecution Database: Historical enforcement patterns
  • Charity Commission: Third-sector intermediary analysis

5. Compliance Framework Alignment

  • UK Ministry of Justice Bribery Act Guidance: §7 adequate procedures (6 principles)
  • SFO Operational Handbook: Investigation triggers
  • ICAEW / ACCA Guidelines: Professional accounting standards

6. Example Detection Scenario

"A FTSE 250 engineering firm wins a £50M Middle East contract through a local 'consultant.' UK Bribery Act requires due diligence on that consultant. AGENT scans Companies House — consultant was incorporated 6 days before the contract, dissolved 3 months after payment. Address matches 47 other shell entities. Reciprocity test shows the procurement director's brother is a director of the consultancy. Report: 'SECTION_7_RISK HIGH — no adequate procedures defence available under UK Bribery Act.'"
