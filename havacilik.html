AGENT_AVIATION_CM — Aviation Component Counterfeit Detection

Risk: FAA estimates 1-2% of aviation parts are counterfeit. Safety-critical: engines, avionics, flight controls.

1. Mission

Detect counterfeit aircraft components in the supply chain through structural genealogy testing, distributor chain verification, and physical-digital coherence analysis.

2. Detection Architecture

2.1 Part Number Genealogy

  • G1 — Part Number Anomaly: Part numbers not matching OEM format or containing typographical variations
  • G2 — Serial Number Gap: Serial numbers out of known production range
  • G3 — Date Code Inconsistency: Manufacturing date codes inconsistent with part design introduction date

2.2 Distributor Chain Verification

  • D1 — Distributor Authorization: Claimed OEM authorization vs. actual OEM distributor list
  • D2 — Chain of Custody Gap: Missing intermediary in documented sales chain
  • D3 — Cross-Reference Test: Same part tracked through multiple distributor claims

2.3 Physical-Digital Coherence

  • P1 — Weight/Dimension: Declared vs. measured physical properties
  • P2 — Surface Finish: Manufacturing quality markers inconsistent with OEM standards
  • P3 — Documentation Test: Certificate of Conformity format/quality vs. OEM standard

3. Proprietary Indicators

Indicator Description Threshold
BOGUS_P counterfeiting probability score >0.7 = SUSPEND_PART
CHAIN_G supply chain completeness <0.8 = INVESTIGATE
AUTH_C OEM-authorization match FALSE = HIGH_RISK

AGENT_AVIATION_PROD — Aircraft Production Integrity Module

Market: Airbus + Boeing combined production ~1,500 aircraft/yr, $150B revenue Key Players: Airbus (Toulouse), Boeing (Seattle, Charleston), Spirit Aerosystems, Collins, Safran

1. Mission

Structural integrity testing of aircraft production. Detect quality deviations, production rate manipulation, FAI fraud, and supply chain quality anomalies in aerospace manufacturing.

2. Detection Architecture

2.1 Production Rate Anomaly

  • R1 — Rate vs. Capability: Claimed production rate vs. physical factory capacity, headcount, shift hours
  • R2 — Quality-Rate Correlation: As rates increase, quality defects should increase. If not reported = suppression
  • R3 — Delivery Lag Analysis: Production claim date vs. actual first flight/delivery date gap

2.2 FAI (First Article Inspection) Integrity

  • F1 — FAI Completeness: AS9102 FAI form fields vs. actual inspection records
  • F2 — Dimensional Compliance: Claimed vs. measured dimensions for critical features
  • F3 — Material Certification: Claimed material specs vs. supplier certificates

2.3 Deviation/Concession Forensics

  • D1 — Deviation Rate Tracking: Number of engineering deviations per production batch vs. historical baseline
  • D2 — Concession Approval Pattern: Same deviations repeatedly approved = systemic quality gap
  • D3 — Repair vs. Replace: Structural repairs on production line vs. part replacement ratio

3. Proprietary Indicators

Indicator Description Threshold
PROD_RATE claimed_rate / physical_capacity >0.9 = WARNING, >1.0 = IMPOSSIBLE
QUALITY_Q expected_defects - reported_defects >15% = SUPPRESSION
FAI_C completed_fields / required_fields <0.95 = INCOMPLETE

AGENT_AVIATION_ENGINE — Aircraft Engine MRO Forensics Module

Market: Engine MRO ~$30B/yr segment of total $80-90B MRO market UK Connection: Rolls-Royce (Derby, Bristol) - major UK employer, critical export Key Players: Rolls-Royce, GE Aviation, Pratt & Whitney, CFM International, IAE, MTU, Lufthansa Technik

1. Mission

Structural integrity testing of engine shop visit claims, performance restoration verification, and Life Limited Part tracking.

2. Detection Architecture

2.1 Shop Visit Claim Verification

  • S1 — Workscope vs. Performed: Contracted workscope vs. actual modules disassembled/inspected
  • S2 — Part Replacement Rate: Parts replaced vs. manufacturer recommended limits. Above threshold = overbilling
  • S3 — Repair vs. Replace: Repairable parts replaced instead of repaired = revenue optimization fraud

2.2 Performance Restoration Claims

  • P1 — EGT Margin Improvement: Claimed exhaust gas temperature margin vs. pre/post shop visit data
  • P2 — Fuel Flow Improvement: Specific fuel consumption change vs. workscope
  • P3 — Performance Restoration Guarantee: Guarantee vs. actual performance retention

2.3 Life Limited Part (LLP) Tracking

  • L1 — Cycle/Time Accuracy: Claimed cycles/hours vs. aircraft utilization records
  • L2 — LLP Trace Integrity: Full traceability from birth to retirement
  • L3 — LLP Extensions: Unauthorized life extension beyond manufacturer limits

2.4 Borescope Fraud Detection

  • B1 — Image Timestamp: Borescope image metadata vs. inspection date
  • B2 — Consecutive Inspection Consistency: Same damage progressing vs. appearing suddenly
  • B3 — Known Damage Concealment: Critical findings not documented

3. Proprietary Indicators

Indicator Description Threshold
SV_WORKSCOPE performed_scope / contracted_scope <0.9 = INCOMPLETE
EGT_G claimed_egt_improvement - actual >10°C = MISREPRESENTED
LLP_C documented_cycles / actual_utilization >1.05 = OVERSTATED

AGENT_AVIATION_MRO — Aircraft MRO Fraud & Integrity Module

Market: Global MRO market $80-90B/yr (2025) Regulatory: EASA Part 145, FAA Part 43/145, UK CAA ANO Key Players: Lufthansa Technik, GE Services, Rolls-Royce, Air France KLM MRO, HAECO, ST Engineering

1. Mission

Structural integrity testing of aircraft maintenance operations. Detect phantom repairs, billing fraud, unnecessary part replacement, and record tampering in the MRO ecosystem.

2. Detection Architecture

2.1 Phantom Repair Detection

  • P1 — Labor Hour Anomaly: Claimed repair hours vs. industry standard (e.g., "4-engine C-check in 8 hours")
  • P2 — Part Consumption Ratio: Parts billed vs. parts physically consumed. Systematic over-billing = phantom
  • P3 — Task Duplication: Same repair task billed multiple times on different work orders

2.2 Unnecessary Part Replacement

  • U1 — Premature Replacement: LLP/LRU replaced well before manufacturer-recommended limits
  • U2 — Batch Pattern Detection: Same part replaced fleet-wide simultaneously (possible OEM-driven vs. genuine need)
  • U3 — Used Part Integrity: Parts claimed as "new" but exhibiting wear patterns inconsistent with zero hours

2.3 Maintenance Record Tampering

  • T1 — Timestamp Integrity: Work order timestamps vs. actual aircraft availability. Repair claimed while aircraft was flying
  • T2 — Signature Chain: Engineer authorization chain gaps or irregularities
  • T3 — Airworthiness Directive Compliance: AD compliance claimed but part/manual records inconsistent

2.4 MRO Pricing Manipulation

  • M1 — Rate Deviation: Shop rates vs. market benchmark. Systematic overpricing
  • M2 — Scope Creep Detection: "Found during inspection" repairs that follow a pattern
  • M3 — Flat Rate Manipulation: Flat rate vs. actual hour comparison per task type

3. Proprietary Indicators

Indicator Description Threshold
PHANTOM_H claimed_hrs - industry_std_hrs >2σ = PHANTOM
PART_C billed_parts - consumed_parts >5% = INVESTIGATE
PREMATURE_R replacement_ts - mfr_limit_ts <0.7 = PREMATURE
TIMESTAMP_D work_ts - aircraft_available_ts negative = FABRICATED

4. UK Connection

  • UK CAA regulatory alignment
  • Rolls-Royce engine MRO (Derby, Bristol)
  • London Heathrow / Gatwick line maintenance
  • UK MRO providers: Monarch Aircraft Engineering, Marshall Aerospace

2.1 Fatigue Risk Detection

  • F2 — Circadian Disruption: Pattern of early starts, late finishes, and time zone crossings

  • S2 — Reserve Abuse: Reserve called at maximum legal duty to minimize pay
  • S3 — Bid Line Manipulation: Seniority-based bid system integrity

AGENT_AVIATION_FOQA — Flight Data Monitoring Analytics Module

1. Mission

Deep structural analysis of flight data recorder (FDR/QAR) data. Detect exceedance concealment, fuel fraud, approach instability patterns, and safety culture from aggregate flight data.

2. Detection Architecture

2.1 Exceedance Pattern Analysis

  • E1 — Exceedance Concealment: Exceedances in raw data not logged in pilot reports
  • E2 — Recurring Non-Compliance: Same aircraft/pilot/captain repeating same exceedance type
  • E3 — Trending: Exceedance rates increasing over time = safety culture indicator

2.2 Approach/Landing Analysis

  • A1 — Unstable Approach: Speed, altitude, configuration deviations from stabilized approach criteria
  • A2 — Hard Landing Detection: Vertical acceleration + sink rate cross-reference
  • A3 — Go-Around Analysis: Go-around decision timing and altitude

2.3 Fuel Efficiency Forensics

  • F1 — APU Usage Anomaly: APU run time vs. ground power availability
  • F2 — Fuel Tankering: Fuel loaded beyond needed for sector
  • F3 — Optimum Altitude Deviation: Cruising altitude vs. optimum for weight/conditions

3. Proprietary Indicators

Indicator Description Threshold
EXCEED_C raw_exceedances - reported_exceedances >0 = CONCEALMENT
STABLE_R unstable_approaches / total_approaches >5% = RISK
FUEL_D actual_fuel_burn - expected_burn >5% = ANOMALY

AGENT_AVIATION_BAG — Baggage Handling Integrity Module

Market: 4.5B bags checked annually, 0.5% mishandled (~22M bags), $2.5B annual cost Regulatory: IATA Resolution 753 (bag tracking at 4 key points), Montreal Convention (liability) Key Players: SITA, Amadeus, Vanderlande, Daifuku, BCS Group

1. Mission

Baggage handling system structural integrity testing. Detect tracking gaps, mishandling root causes, theft, and systemic failures in baggage operations.

2. Detection Architecture

2.1 RFID Tracking Integrity

  • R1 — Scan Gap Detection: Missing scan at any of IATA 753 mandatory points (check-in, load, transfer, arrival)
  • R2 — Timing Anomaly: Bag scanned at implausible speed between points
  • R3 — Handoff Failure: Bag transferred between carriers but scan chain breaks at handoff

2.2 Mishandling Root Cause Analysis

  • M1 — Transfer Time: Connection time vs. minimum connection time (MCT) gap
  • M2 — Flight-Specific: Particular flights with higher mishandling rates = systemic problem
  • M3 — Station-Correlated: Particular airports/stations with chronic failure patterns

2.3 Baggage Theft/Tampering

  • T1 — Weight Change: Bag weight at check-in vs. arrival. Difference = tampering indicator
  • T2 — Route Deviation: Bag traveled through non-standard path
  • T3 — Access Pattern: Unauthorized baggage system access correlated with loss

3. Proprietary Indicators

Indicator Description Threshold
SCAN_C completed_scans / required_scans <1.0 = GAP
TIMING_D actual_transfer_time - min_required <MCT = RISK
WEIGHT_D arrival_weight - checkin_weight >1kg = TAMPERING
