AGENT_AVIATION_ENGINE — Aircraft Engine MRO Forensics Module

Market: Engine MRO ~$30B/yr segment of total $80-90B MRO market UK Connection: Rolls-Royce (Derby, Bristol) - major UK employer, critical export Key Players: Rolls-Royce, GE Aviation, Pratt & Whitney, CFM International, IAE, MTU, Lufthansa Technik

1. Mission

Structural integrity testing of engine shop visit claims, performance restoration verification, and Life Limited Part tracking.

2. Detection Architecture

2.1 Shop Visit Claim Verification

  • S1 — Workscope vs. Performed: Contracted workscope vs. actual modules disassembled/inspected
  • S2 — Part Replacement Rate: Parts replaced vs. manufacturer recommended limits. Above threshold = overbilling
  • S3 — Repair vs. Replace: Repairable parts replaced instead of repaired = revenue optimization fraud

2.2 Performance Restoration Claims

  • P1 — EGT Margin Improvement: Claimed exhaust gas temperature margin vs. pre/post shop visit data
  • P2 — Fuel Flow Improvement: Specific fuel consumption change vs. workscope
  • P3 — Performance Restoration Guarantee: Guarantee vs. actual performance retention

2.3 Life Limited Part (LLP) Tracking

  • L1 — Cycle/Time Accuracy: Claimed cycles/hours vs. aircraft utilization records
  • L2 — LLP Trace Integrity: Full traceability from birth to retirement
  • L3 — LLP Extensions: Unauthorized life extension beyond manufacturer limits

2.4 Borescope Fraud Detection

  • B1 — Image Timestamp: Borescope image metadata vs. inspection date
  • B2 — Consecutive Inspection Consistency: Same damage progressing vs. appearing suddenly
  • B3 — Known Damage Concealment: Critical findings not documented

3. Proprietary Indicators

Indicator Description Threshold

EGT_G claimed_egt_improvement - actual >10°C = MISREPRESENTED
